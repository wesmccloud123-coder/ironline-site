Opening Sequence

Arrival & First Impression

You start with atmosphere. Not scorecards. Not rate tables. This section should sell elevation, arrival, clubhouse approach, and the feeling of stepping onto protected ground.

Clubhouse Arrival

Frame the driveway, practice green, porte cochère, and first terrace view as a pre-round ritual. Luxury clubs win by selling anticipation before the first shot is even struck.

Aerial Orientation

Use a soft parallax estate view with a restrained overlay map so visitors understand the scale of the property without feeling like they opened a GIS tool.

Hole 07

The Signature Bend

This is where the page gets cinematic. Elevated tee. Long diagonal carry. Tree line tightening the landing zone. Visitors should feel the hole before they understand it.

Design Language

Use a full-width aerial composition, restrained gold labels, and a short narration that explains the decision tree: bold line, conservative line, and the recovery zone if ambition goes wrong.

Member Story

Pair each key hole with a one-line member quote or pro insight. That gives the course character instead of reducing it to slope data and yardage markers.

Closing Stretch
